Flowers

Petals wake with whispers of the dawn,
Soft as breath on a quiet windowpane.
They rise from earth where dreams are drawn,
Drinking light after gentle rain.

A garden hums in silent grace,
Colors bloom without a sound.
Each fragile bloom holds time in place,
Rooted deep in sacred ground.

Crimson speaks of burning hearts,
Golden glows with hopeful cheer.
Violet dreams in secret arts,
White dissolves each trace of fear.

The breeze becomes a tender guide,
Carrying fragrance through the air.
Where blossoms open, worries hide,
And beauty answers every prayer.

In every bud, a promise sleeps,
Wrapped in folds of living art.
Nature its quiet vigil keeps,
Breathing life in every part.

Some flowers bloom for just a day,
Yet leave a memory profound.
Their silent lesson gently says,
True grace asks no trumpet sound.

Dewdrops rest on velvet skin,
Like stars that fell but chose to stay.
Morning light awakens them,
Turning tears to bright display.

Fields of bloom like painted seas,
Waves of color softly flow.
They dance in rhythm with the breeze,
In timeless cycles none can slow.

From tender seed to blossom fair,
A journey written in the sun.
They teach how life renews with care,
And every ending’s just begun.

So pause where quiet flowers grow,
And hear what words cannot declare.
In fragile forms, deep truths they show —
Strength and softness living there.
